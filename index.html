Bicycle Infrastructure

Move People, Not Cars

And Drivers will Benefit Too!

Between an abundance of space and population centers that developed after the widespread adoption of cars, many cities in the United States are designed to move cars, not people.

Drivers, which in the US includes the vast majority of adults, don't automatically realize how beneficial bicycle and pedestrian accomodations are to streamlining automobile traffic.

  • As bike and foot traffic increases, car traffic decreases
  • As bike and pedestrian traffic increases, the space needed to accomodate more people actually DECREASES
  • With fewer cars on the road, traffic flows more smoothly
  • For drivers, traffic time and congestion actually decreases
  • With designated bike and pedestrian facilities, there's no more guess-work for drivers to know where road users will be and how they will use the space
  • The roadway itself makes it clear who, what, and how people will be moving in public space.
  • Drivers will have a lower risk of collision with bicycle riders and pedestrians. People outside of cars will have safer networks to use.

Let the positive CYCLE of moving more people begin!
